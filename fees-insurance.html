How to read this table

These are private-pay reference rates. They help clients understand the cost of services when insurance is not being used, when a service is not covered, or while insurance participation is still being confirmed.

When insurance applies

Once Stonebridge is active with a plan, eligible psychotherapy services may be billed through insurance. Your out-of-pocket cost may be lower than the private-pay rate, depending on your plan.

Why this is stated clearly

Clear financial information is part of ethical care. Fees, billing arrangements, limits of insurance, and client responsibilities should be discussed early enough that people can make informed decisions.

What You Receive

What the appointment includes.

The fee is for a structured psychotherapy appointment, not only the minutes spent speaking on video. The structure is intended to give clients a steady, reliable frame for care.

Reserved clinical time

Psychotherapy appointments are scheduled in 60-minute blocks. The direct clinical portion of a standard session typically lasts 45–50 minutes.

Care between the lines

The remaining time supports documentation, treatment planning, transition, and preparation so the work can stay organized and clinically responsible.

A steady therapy frame

Clear appointment length, payment expectations, consent, confidentiality limits, and attendance policies help therapy feel predictable rather than improvised.

Insurance

Insurance and payment.

Stonebridge is awaiting confirmation of insurance paneling with select commercial plans. Insurance billing begins only after participation is active, formally confirmed, and benefits are verified.

During paneling

The listed fees provide the clearest private-pay reference while insurance participation is being confirmed.

When active

If Stonebridge is in network with your plan and the service is eligible, claims may be submitted on your behalf.

Your responsibility

Clients may still be responsible for copays, deductibles, coinsurance, private-pay services, non-covered services, missed appointment fees, or balances after insurance processing.

Verification of benefits can help estimate coverage, but it is not a guarantee of payment. Final responsibility depends on the plan, claim processing, signed financial agreements, and applicable law or contract requirements.
Policies

Payment details.

These policies are stated plainly so clients know what to expect before treatment begins.

Out-of-network benefits Some clients may be eligible for partial reimbursement depending on their plan.
Superbills A superbill may be available upon request when appropriate for private-pay services.
Missed appointments Insurance cannot be billed for missed appointments or late cancellations because no covered session occurred.
24-hour notice Late cancellations or no-shows may be charged the applicable session fee unless an exception is approved.

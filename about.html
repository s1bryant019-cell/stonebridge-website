Built for careful work.

Client Experience

What clients should feel here.

Stonebridge is designed to feel calm, serious, and human. The goal is not to make therapy feel formal for its own sake. The goal is to give clients a reliable place where the work can become clearer over time.

A careful first step

A brief note is enough. You do not need to explain everything perfectly before Stonebridge reviews fit, scope, telehealth appropriateness, and next steps.

A steady therapy frame

Clients can expect clear appointment structure, privacy expectations, payment information, consent forms, and communication boundaries before treatment begins.

A whole-person view

Symptoms matter, but they are not the whole story. Therapy should make room for history, relationships, identity, coping, grief, trauma, self-worth, and meaning.

Honest direction

If another provider, specialty, crisis resource, or level of care is a better fit, Stonebridge may offer referral guidance when possible.

How It Works

The model in ordinary steps.

The care model is intentionally simple from the client side. The structure behind the scenes exists so the beginning of therapy is thoughtful rather than rushed or improvised.

Step 01

Submit a brief inquiry

Share basic contact information and a short note about what you are hoping therapy may help address.

Step 02

Fit is reviewed

Stonebridge considers whether outpatient telehealth psychotherapy may be clinically appropriate and within the practice’s scope.

Step 03

Next steps are clarified

If the practice appears appropriate, Stonebridge follows up about availability, insurance status, consultation, and possible intake paperwork.

Why Structure Matters

Warmth and clarity belong together.

Good therapy offers a steady relationship where a person can feel known more fully. That steadiness depends on more than kindness. It also depends on clear consent, confidentiality, scope, payment expectations, communication boundaries, and responsible referral decisions.

Consent Clients should understand the nature of therapy, the limits of the setting, and the practical frame before ongoing care begins.
Confidentiality Privacy matters. The limits of confidentiality are explained so clients know how information is protected and when disclosure may be required.
Competence Stonebridge considers whether the concern fits the practice’s scope instead of accepting every inquiry simply because someone is ready to schedule.
Fees Fees, insurance status, billing limits, and client responsibility should be stated clearly enough for informed decisions.
Scope

What Stonebridge is for.

Stonebridge’s active care pathway is outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for teens and adults located in Illinois. The practice is designed for clients seeking careful, relational, clinically responsible therapy rather than open booking or high-volume access.

Appropriate for the current pathway

  • Outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for teens and adults in Illinois.
  • Clients seeking depth, steadiness, and a thoughtful relationship with a clinician.
  • Concerns that can be responsibly addressed through outpatient psychotherapy.
  • People who value clear expectations around time, communication, privacy, and fees.

Better served elsewhere

  • Immediate crisis or emergency needs requiring urgent response.
  • Needs requiring in-person care, intensive services, or a higher level of care.
  • Requests outside Stonebridge’s active psychotherapy consultation pathway.
  • Assessment and forensic services use dedicated procedures beyond this pathway.

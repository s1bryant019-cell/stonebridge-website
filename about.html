About Stonebridge

A practice built for careful, relational care.

Stonebridge Psychological Group was created to offer outpatient telehealth psychotherapy in a setting that feels steady, thoughtful, and clinically clear. The practice is consultation-first because fit, scope, and pacing matter before care begins.

Practice Identity

A steadier frame for therapy.

Many people come to therapy already feeling rushed, overwhelmed, or unsure whether anyone is really listening. The practice frame should reduce confusion, not add to it.

Stonebridge is designed around fit, steady communication, responsible limits, and the belief that therapy works best when the frame is clear enough to hold difficult human work.

Fit over volume The goal is not to fill every available slot. The goal is to make thoughtful decisions about whether Stonebridge is the right setting.
Consultation before scheduling Beginning care includes a fit-based review rather than immediate self-scheduling. That protects clients from being placed into care that may not match their needs.
Values in practice Values become meaningful through real decisions: scheduling, scope, communication, consent, documentation, referrals, and clinical boundaries.
“

The business exists to support people’s lives, not become their lives.

This principle applies to the people seeking care and to the clinicians providing it. Stonebridge is designed so care can be thoughtful, ethical, and sustainable rather than organized around constant overextension.

The Practice Frame

The frame around care matters.

Stonebridge treats the structure of the practice as part of clinical care. A clear frame reduces confusion, protects privacy, supports good judgment, and makes it easier to be honest about fit.

Attention Therapy requires emotional presence, reflection, and clinical judgment. Stonebridge is not designed around endless availability or a rushed calendar.
Fit The concern, age range, risk level, telehealth format, and clinician expertise need to make sense before therapy begins.
Boundaries Clear expectations around time, communication, attendance, privacy, and emergencies protect the therapy relationship rather than distance it.
Integrity When Stonebridge is not the right setting, the more ethical answer is to say that clearly and help point the person toward a better-fit level or type of care.

Honest direction

Current Scope

Current scope.

Stonebridge’s current active care pathway is outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for teens and adults located in Illinois.

Stonebridge is not an emergency or crisis service and does not use open booking. When a different level or type of care is needed, referral direction may be discussed when possible. Assessment and forensic services use dedicated procedures beyond the psychotherapy consultation pathway.

For clinicians

Monthly consultation for thoughtful outpatient work.

Stonebridge hosts a monthly consultation space for mental health clinicians interested in careful, relational, ethically grounded outpatient work.

The group is open to clinicians outside Stonebridge. Participation is reviewed for fit, scope, and group composition before joining.

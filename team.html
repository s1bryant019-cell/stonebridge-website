What patients should be able to see here.

A team page should not feel like a roster or a hiring page. It should help a prospective client understand who may be involved in their care and whether the setting feels appropriate.

A person, not a slot

You should be able to see who is leading the practice, how the clinician describes the work, and whether the tone feels steady enough to begin.

A clear clinical frame

Stonebridge uses a consultation-first pathway rather than open booking so fit, scope, telehealth appropriateness, and next steps can be clarified before care begins.

A responsible next step

A waitlist inquiry is a starting point, not a guarantee of treatment. If Stonebridge appears clinically appropriate, the practice follows up regarding availability and consultation.

Fit matters because care is personal.

Stonebridge does not treat clinicians as interchangeable. A therapy match should make sense for the client’s needs, the clinician’s scope, the telehealth setting, and the level of care required. This is part of ethical, patient-friendly care.

What is reviewed Presenting concerns, age, location in Illinois, telehealth fit, urgency, risk level, insurance/payment questions, and whether outpatient psychotherapy appears appropriate.
What is not promised A waitlist inquiry does not establish a clinical relationship, guarantee an appointment, or replace emergency or crisis care.
When referral may be better If a different specialty, in-person care, urgent support, or a higher level of care is needed, Stonebridge may recommend another provider or setting.

Credentials and training.

Formal credential language matters. Stonebridge keeps the public profile clear without overstating licensure, scope, or specialization.

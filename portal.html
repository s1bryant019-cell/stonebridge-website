The portal is intended for active Stonebridge clients. If you are not yet established with the practice, begin with a waitlist inquiry.

Active clients

Portal access is used after consultation, consent, and intake steps are underway or complete.

Assigned materials

Forms and questionnaires are assigned based on clinical relevance and stage of care.

Routine use

The portal supports routine care coordination. It is not an emergency or crisis contact method.

What the portal is for

A practical place for client care tasks.

TherapyPortal keeps routine administrative steps connected to care without making the website itself the place for private clinical details.

Paperwork Complete assigned intake materials, consent forms, releases, and other clinically relevant forms when requested.
Appointments Review appointment-related details and access scheduled telehealth sessions when portal access has been activated.
Billing items Manage billing-related items when available through the portal, depending on account setup and practice workflow.
Secure access Use the portal for assigned routine tasks rather than sending sensitive information through the public website.

Before you use it

The portal supports care after the practice has connected with you.

New prospective clients should not use the portal as the first step. Stonebridge begins with a waitlist inquiry and fit review before intake paperwork or portal tasks are assigned.

Not yet a client?

If you are interested in beginning outpatient telehealth psychotherapy, use the waitlist form instead of the portal.

Once Stonebridge has reviewed fit and next steps, assigned paperwork may be sent through TherapyPortal when clinically appropriate.

Not for urgent or emergency needs.

The portal is not monitored as an emergency service. For immediate danger or a medical emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. For mental health, suicide, self-harm, or substance-use crisis support, call or text 988.

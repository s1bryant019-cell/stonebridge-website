Psychological Assessment

Assessment for clarity and direction.

Psychological assessment at Stonebridge is intended to bring greater clarity to diagnosis, formulation, and treatment planning. The goal is thoughtful understanding, not just a label.

Why assessment may be useful

Assessment can help clarify patterns that have been difficult to understand in treatment, distinguish between overlapping concerns, and create a stronger clinical picture when next steps feel uncertain.

How it is approached

The process is structured and clinically grounded, with attention to referral questions, relevant history, testing where appropriate, and recommendations that are intended to be genuinely useful.

Possible reasons for referral

Diagnostic clarification, personality functioning, attention or executive concerns, mood and anxiety patterns, or treatment planning questions.

What the process may include

Clinical interview, review of history, testing measures when indicated, and an integrative written or verbal feedback process.

Outcome

A clearer framework for understanding the person’s psychological functioning and what kind of care may be most helpful moving forward.

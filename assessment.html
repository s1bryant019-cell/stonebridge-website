Psychological Assessment

Assessment uses a separate clinical pathway.

Psychological assessment differs from psychotherapy and requires its own referral review, testing procedures, interpretation, documentation, consent, and feedback process.

Return to Services

Separate review

Assessment begins with referral clarification rather than the psychotherapy waitlist.

Dedicated procedures

Testing, interpretation, written work, and feedback require a distinct service frame.

Assessment scope

A defined process before any testing begins.

The referral question, purpose, role, records, consent, and practical requirements must be clear before assessment services can be considered.

01 · Referral

Clarify the question first.

Assessment starts by identifying what is being asked, who will use the findings, and whether the request fits the available professional role.

02 · Procedures

Testing requires a dedicated frame.

Measures, interviews, records, interpretation, documentation, and fees are determined by the referral question rather than by the psychotherapy process.

03 · Feedback

Findings require careful communication.

Feedback and written work are handled according to the agreed purpose, consent, audience, and documentation boundaries.

Current availability is reviewed separately.

This is not part of the standard public psychotherapy pathway. Specific professional inquiries may be directed to Stonebridge by email.

Psychological Assessment

Assessment as a future service pathway.

Psychological assessment remains part of the broader Stonebridge practice model, but it is not currently available for new requests. This page describes the intended clinical approach for future assessment services.

Current availability

Stonebridge is not currently accepting psychological assessment requests. Psychotherapy inquiries may still be submitted through the consultation form.

What assessment can support

When available, assessment may be useful when there are important questions about diagnosis, emotional functioning, personality structure, or the broader clinical picture. It can also help organize treatment planning and bring more clarity to complicated presentations.

Diagnostic clarification
Clinical understanding and formulation
Recommendations for treatment planning

Reasons someone may seek assessment

Assessment may be requested when symptoms are unclear, when treatment has stalled, when diagnostic questions remain unresolved, or when a more structured evaluation would be clinically helpful.

How the process is approached

The assessment process is intended to be careful, clinically grounded, and well organized. The aim is not simply to produce labels, but to arrive at a more meaningful understanding of the person and the relevant clinical questions.

Current status

Stonebridge is not currently accepting assessment inquiries. The consultation form will indicate that this service is unavailable if assessment is selected.

A more thoughtful evaluation process

Not every referral question requires the same kind of assessment. When assessment becomes available, Stonebridge will approach evaluation with attention to scope, clarity, and clinically useful recommendations.

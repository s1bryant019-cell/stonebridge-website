Professional support for complex clinical work.

Stonebridge offers consultation groups, individual consultation, external supervision, psychological assessment report support, scholarly review, and burnout-focused professional education.

Professional offerings

Defined services with a clear purpose and frame.

Each request is reviewed for role, scope, timing, confidentiality, intended use, and whether Stonebridge is the appropriate professional fit.

Interested in the monthly consultation group?

The group meets on the third Friday of each month at 4:00 PM Central Time by secure video. Share your professional role, practice setting, and what you hope to bring to the group.

Ask to Join the Group
01

Monthly consultation group

A consistent forum for mental-health clinicians inside and outside Stonebridge who want thoughtful discussion of difficult outpatient work.

  • Third Friday of each month
  • 4:00 PM Central Time
  • Held by secure video
  • Group composition reviewed before participation
02

Individual clinical consultation and peer consultation

Focused discussion for psychologists, counselors, trainees, and other mental-health professionals seeking support with case conceptualization, differential diagnosis, treatment planning, trauma, addiction, co-occurring presentations, risk, ethics, boundaries, relational dynamics, countertransference, documentation, or sustainable practice.

03

External clinical supervision

Structured supervision may be available to eligible counselors and trainees who are not employed by Stonebridge. Work may include case conceptualization, treatment planning, documentation, ethics, risk assessment, use of self, professional identity, boundaries, workload, and burnout prevention.

Formal supervision is offered only when Dr. Bryant’s qualifications, the supervisee’s professional pathway, the work setting, and applicable requirements are compatible. A written agreement and clear responsibility structure are required.

04

Psychological assessment report drafting and review

Professional writing support for psychologists and assessment practices that need assistance transforming authorized referral information, interviews, records, behavioral observations, test data, and clinical findings into a clear, integrated psychological report.

  • Drafting from supplied assessment materials
  • Integrating history, findings, formulation, and recommendations
  • Revising structure, clarity, and coherence
  • Reviewing a draft before finalization

The requesting evaluator remains responsible for the assessment, independent review of all materials, final interpretations and conclusions, signature, and release of the report. Secure transfer, authorization, and confidentiality requirements must be established before work begins.

05

Manuscript, article, and dissertation review

Structured conceptual and editorial feedback for psychology-related scholarly or professional work, including journal manuscripts, article drafts, dissertation proposals or chapters, literature integration, argument development, clinical accuracy, methodological coherence, APA-style presentation, and responses to reviewer or committee feedback.

Review services provide feedback and professional consultation. They do not include ghostwriting, authorship misrepresentation, or completing academic requirements on another person’s behalf.

06

Burnout training, webinars, and continuing education

Professional education may be developed for clinicians, supervisors, treatment programs, and organizations seeking a more serious understanding of burnout, secondary trauma, workforce sustainability, and the conditions that shape clinical effectiveness.

  • Beyond burnout: individual and organizational factors
  • Secondary trauma, compassion fatigue, and vicarious trauma
  • Sustainable clinical practice, caseloads, and boundaries
  • Burnout and workforce stress in addiction treatment
  • Supervisors and organizations as burnout interventions
  • Custom presentations by audience and setting

Formal continuing-education credit is represented only when the event is offered through an appropriate approved sponsor or qualifying collaboration.

Professional scope

Clear roles protect the clinician, the client, and the work.

Professional services are separate from psychotherapy and are defined through the requested role, intended use, and applicable responsibilities.

Evaluator responsibility

Report support does not transfer responsibility for assessment procedures, data, interpretation, diagnosis, conclusions, signature, or release from the requesting evaluator.

Scholarly integrity

Article, manuscript, and dissertation review provides conceptual and editorial feedback rather than ghostwriting or completion of another person’s academic work.

Supervision eligibility

External supervision depends on professional eligibility, role clarity, a written agreement, documentation expectations, and compatibility with the supervisee’s training or licensure pathway.

Continuing education

CE credit is identified only when the event is offered through an appropriate approved sponsor or qualifying collaboration.

Consultation limits

Consultation is not psychotherapy, emergency response, legal advice, or a substitute for an employer’s, institution’s, licensing board’s, or attorney’s responsibilities.

How I work

Therapy with Dr. Bryant is primarily insight-oriented. The work looks beneath immediate symptoms to understand recurring emotional patterns, relationship templates, losses, conflicts, defenses, and ways of coping that may have once been protective but now feel limiting.

His approach is grounded in psychodynamic, relational, trauma-informed, and self-psychological therapy. Practical tools from CBT, DBT, ACT, motivational interviewing, and mindfulness may be integrated when structure, coping skills, behavior change, or emotional regulation would support the deeper work.

The goal is to help clients understand themselves more clearly, feel less controlled by old patterns, and develop more flexible ways of relating to themselves and others.

What therapy may look like

Sessions are conversational, reflective, and active. A session may begin with anxiety, grief, conflict, substance use concerns, burnout, trauma reminders, or feeling disconnected from oneself.

The work often slows down a reaction, conflict, urge, or emotional shift and asks what it means, what it protects, and what feels difficult to say directly.

Some sessions may focus on current life. Others may move more deeply into history, identity, shame, loss, attachment, or patterns that have been difficult to understand alone.

Training, credentials, and clinical background

Dr. Bryant’s background includes doctoral training in clinical psychology, graduate training in counseling and forensic psychology, and licensure as a Licensed Clinical Professional Counselor in Illinois.

His training also includes 2 master’s degrees in clinically related fields, trauma-focused certification, psychological assessment experience, and clinical work across multiple levels of behavioral health care.

Those settings have included outpatient private practice, community mental health, intensive outpatient treatment, partial hospitalization, inpatient behavioral health, substance use treatment, psychological assessment, supervision, and interdisciplinary treatment planning.

His broader professional work has included trauma-focused care, professional education, supervision of doctoral-level trainees, and research related to clinician burnout and sustainable clinical practice.

Clinical perspective

Dr. Bryant’s experience across outpatient and higher-acuity settings informs careful attention to clinical fit, risk level, diagnostic complexity, and the limits of outpatient telehealth care.

Stonebridge is designed for thoughtful outpatient psychotherapy. When another level of care or a different service would better fit the client’s needs, referral guidance may be offered when possible.

Before reaching out

A brief consultation helps determine whether Stonebridge is an appropriate place to begin. Consultation is not emergency care and does not establish a clinical relationship.

Psychological testing, forensic evaluation, legal-system services, emergency care, and higher levels of care require different procedures and may not be available through a psychotherapy consultation.

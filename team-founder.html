Accepting psychotherapy inquiries

Stephen W. Bryant, PsyD, LCPC

Founder · Telehealth psychotherapy for adults in Illinois

People often reach out when they are carrying more than they can comfortably hold alone — stress, anxiety, burnout, relationship patterns, grief, trauma history, substance use concerns, or major transitions that have started to affect daily life.

Therapy with Stephen is intended to be thoughtful, steady, and collaborative. The work begins with understanding what is happening, what has helped before, what has not, and whether outpatient telehealth is the right level of care.

People often reach out when…

  • They look functional on the outside but feel overwhelmed, depleted, or disconnected internally.
  • Old coping strategies are no longer working as well as they once did.
  • Relationships, work, identity, grief, or transition points have become difficult to sort through alone.
  • They want therapy that can hold complexity without rushing to generic advice.

My style

Stephen’s style is collaborative and psychologically curious. Sessions may include reflection, pattern recognition, emotional processing, practical coping, and attention to the parts of life that feel difficult to name clearly.

Good fit

A good fit may include adults seeking outpatient psychotherapy who can attend telehealth sessions privately, are not in immediate crisis, and want a therapy process that is steady, reflective, and clinically grounded.

Not the right fit

Stonebridge is not appropriate for immediate danger, active emergency needs, mandated forensic work, current psychological assessment requests, or situations requiring in-person or higher-level care.

Professional background

Stephen’s background informs a practice model that treats clinical fit, ethical boundaries, and sustainability as part of quality care. The goal is not to accept every request; it is to help determine whether the care available is appropriate.

Next step

Start with a brief request.

The consultation request is not therapy and does not establish a clinical relationship. It is a first step to help determine whether Stonebridge may be an appropriate outpatient telehealth fit.

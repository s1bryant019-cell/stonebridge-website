Founder Profile

Dr. Stephen W. Bryant

PsyD, LCPC · Founder · Doctoral-level clinician

Dr. Bryant provides outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for teens and adults in Illinois. His work is relational, insight-oriented, trauma-informed, and grounded in careful attention to clinical fit.

Works with

Teens and adults seeking outpatient psychotherapy in Illinois.

Common concerns

Anxiety, depression, trauma responses, grief, relationships, shame, life transitions, identity, and emotional regulation.

Clinical style

Steady, collaborative, relational, and insight-oriented, with practical tools used when they support the work.

Therapy style

How therapy with Dr. Bryant tends to work.

The work is not only about reducing symptoms. It is also about understanding what those symptoms may be connected to.

Sessions often look at what is happening now and how earlier experiences, relationships, losses, coping strategies, and self-protective patterns may still be shaping life. The pace is collaborative and attentive to readiness, safety, and the client’s goals.

Relational The therapy relationship is treated as part of the work, not just the container for it.
Insight-oriented Patterns, feelings, defenses, needs, and meanings are explored so experience becomes more understandable.
Practical when useful CBT, DBT, ACT, motivational interviewing, and mindfulness-informed tools may be integrated when they help.

Fit and scope

What this pathway is, and what it is not.

Stonebridge uses consultation before intake because not every concern is best served by the same setting, level of care, or clinician.

Dr. Bryant currently provides outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for teens and adults in Illinois. The consultation process helps determine whether Stonebridge appears clinically appropriate before care begins.

This pathway is not emergency care, crisis response, psychological testing, forensic evaluation, court-related evaluation, or a substitute for a higher level of care when that is clinically needed. Assessment and forensic services use dedicated procedures outside the psychotherapy waitlist pathway.

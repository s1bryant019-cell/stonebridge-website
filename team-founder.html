About Dr. Bryant

Dr. Bryant is a doctoral-level clinician and Licensed Clinical Professional Counselor with training in clinical psychology, counseling, and forensic psychology. His clinical background includes outpatient private practice, community mental health, intensive outpatient care, partial hospitalization, inpatient behavioral health, psychological assessment, supervision, and interdisciplinary treatment planning.

Across these settings, he has worked with people navigating trauma, substance use, mood and anxiety concerns, neurodevelopmental differences, relationship strain, identity questions, grief, and periods of significant life disruption. At Stonebridge, his current work is focused on outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for adults located in Illinois.

Treatment approach

Therapy with Dr. Bryant is primarily insight-driven. Rather than focusing only on immediate symptom relief, the work looks beneath the surface to understand recurring emotional patterns, relationship templates, defenses, losses, conflicts, and self-protective strategies.

His approach is grounded in psychodynamic, relational, trauma-informed, and self-psychological therapy. Practical tools from CBT, DBT, ACT, motivational interviewing, and mindfulness may be used when structure, coping skills, behavior change, or emotional regulation would support the deeper work.

What therapy may look like

Sessions are conversational, reflective, and active. You may begin with something happening now — anxiety, grief, trauma reminders, substance use concerns, relationship stress, burnout, or feeling disconnected from yourself — but the work often asks what is underneath the immediate problem.

Together, you may slow down a reaction, conflict, urge, or emotional shift and ask: Why does this keep happening? What does this protect? What feels difficult to say directly? What parts of the self have had to stay hidden, defended, or overcontrolled?

Some sessions may feel practical and focused on current life. Others may move more deeply into history, identity, shame, loss, attachment, or patterns that have been difficult to understand alone. The goal is for insight to become emotionally meaningful enough that new choices become possible.

Professional background

Dr. Bryant’s broader professional background includes psychological assessment, trauma-focused treatment, substance use care, supervision of doctoral-level trainees, professional education, and research on clinician burnout. This informs the structure of Stonebridge, which emphasizes thoughtful care, ethical boundaries, and sustainable clinical practice.

Psychological assessment and forensic services are part of Dr. Bryant’s professional background, but Stonebridge is not currently accepting new assessment or forensic service requests.

When a different kind of care may be needed

Stonebridge provides outpatient telehealth psychotherapy. Some concerns may require a higher level of care, in-person treatment, emergency support, psychological testing, forensic evaluation, or a specialized treatment program.

If another setting would be more appropriate, that does not mean your concerns are too much. It means the care should match the need. When possible, referral guidance can be offered.

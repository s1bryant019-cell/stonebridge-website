Founder Profile

Dr. Stephen W. Bryant

PsyD, LCPC · Founder

Dr. Bryant provides individual, couples, and family psychotherapy for adolescents and adults, along with parent support. His work is psychodynamic and relational at the foundation, with evidence-based approaches integrated for trauma, addiction, mood and anxiety concerns, emotional regulation, relationships, and complex co-occurring patterns.

Clinical focus

Areas of clinical depth.

Dr. Bryant has particular experience in three areas while maintaining a broader outpatient practice.

01

Addiction and co-occurring concerns

Substance use, relapse and recovery patterns, ambivalence, process addictions, shame, coping cycles, and dual-diagnosis concerns.

02

Trauma, PTSD, and complex stress

Threat responses, dissociation, safety, emotional regulation, body-based distress, self-worth, and protective patterns.

03

Mood, anxiety, and emotional regulation

Depression, panic, overthinking, irritability, shutdown, mood instability, avoidance, and difficulty feeling steady.

Broader care may include

Relationship patterns, couples and family concerns, identity and self-worth, grief, life transitions, neurodevelopmental concerns, family stress, and parent support.

Clinical approach

How Dr. Bryant works.

Psychodynamic and relational at the foundation, with evidence-based and skills-focused methods integrated according to the client’s concerns, goals, readiness, and needs of the moment.

Good therapy needs a steady frame: enough structure to feel safe, and enough room to be fully human.
01

Psychodynamic and relational psychotherapy

Explores recurring emotional and relationship patterns, defenses, unmet needs, identity, self-worth, and the ways earlier experiences continue to shape present life. The therapeutic relationship itself may help patterns become clearer and more workable.

02

CBT, ACT, and DBT

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy may be used to examine thoughts, behaviors, avoidance, and practical patterns. Acceptance and Commitment Therapy supports values-based action and psychological flexibility. Dialectical Behavior Therapy skills may support emotion regulation, distress tolerance, mindfulness, and interpersonal effectiveness.

03

Motivational interviewing and addiction-focused work

Motivational interviewing supports honest exploration of ambivalence, readiness, substance use, recovery, coping patterns, and change without relying on confrontation or pressure.

04

Trauma-informed, attachment-based, and family-systems work

These approaches connect symptoms with safety, protective responses, relationships, development, and family patterns. Parent or family work may draw on attachment and systems concepts when clinically appropriate.

These approaches are not applied as a fixed formula. Dr. Bryant develops an individualized formulation and draws from different methods when they support the purpose of therapy.

Scope of care

Stonebridge is not an emergency, crisis, psychological-testing, or forensic service. When another type or level of care is more appropriate, referral direction may be discussed when possible.

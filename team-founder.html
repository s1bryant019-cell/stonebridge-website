Founder Profile

Dr. Stephen W. Bryant

PsyD, LCPC · Founder · Doctoral-level clinician

Dr. Bryant provides insight-oriented psychotherapy for teens and adults in Illinois. His clinical work is shaped by doctoral training in clinical psychology, graduate training in clinical and forensic psychology, psychological assessment experience, and work across outpatient, intensive outpatient, partial hospitalization, and inpatient behavioral health settings.

“Therapy should be steady, thoughtful, and human. I want clients to feel taken seriously without feeling pathologized, rushed, or reduced to a diagnosis.”

Clinical path

The clinical path behind Stonebridge.

Stonebridge grew out of clinical work in settings where people carried real complexity and where the frame around care mattered.

Dr. Bryant’s training and experience have included outpatient psychotherapy, community mental health, intensive outpatient treatment, partial hospitalization, inpatient behavioral health, psychological assessment, substance use treatment, trauma-focused care, supervision, professional education, and interdisciplinary treatment planning.

Those settings made clear that clinical skill is only one part of care. The structure around care also matters: referral fit, pace, risk assessment, documentation, communication, boundaries, and whether outpatient therapy is the right level of support.

Stonebridge is an effort to build a practice where the frame is designed on purpose: careful enough to protect depth, clear enough to protect boundaries, and human enough to hold complexity.

The goal is to make the work clinically responsible, sustainable, and real.

Therapy style

How Dr. Bryant works clinically.

Therapy with Dr. Bryant is primarily insight-oriented. The work looks beneath immediate symptoms to understand the patterns and protections that organize a person’s inner life.

Sessions may focus on anxiety, trauma responses, grief, substance use concerns, identity, shame, relationship patterns, self-worth, life transitions, and the parts of a person that learned to survive under pressure. The work may also examine defenses, attachment patterns, losses, conflicts, and ways of coping that once helped but now feel limiting.

His approach is grounded in psychodynamic, relational, trauma-informed, and self-psychological therapy. Practical tools from CBT, DBT, ACT, motivational interviewing, and mindfulness may be integrated when structure, coping skills, behavior change, or emotional regulation would support the deeper work.

The aim is not to force insight or perform depth. The aim is to create enough steadiness that complexity can be understood without reducing the client to symptoms, habits, or diagnosis alone.

Stonebridge focuses on outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for teens and adults in Illinois. Consultation helps determine fit before care begins. Psychological testing, forensic evaluation, legal-system services, emergency care, and higher levels of care require separate procedures and may not be available through a psychotherapy consultation.

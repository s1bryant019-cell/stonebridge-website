What psychotherapy is.

Psychotherapy is a regular, private conversation with a trained clinician. It gives you a place to slow down, understand what is happening, and work toward change with someone who is paying careful attention.

What happens in therapy?

You talk about what has been difficult, what you have tried, what keeps repeating, and what you want to understand or change. The clinician listens for symptoms, patterns, relationships, history, coping, strengths, risks, and goals.

Therapy may include reflection, emotional work, practical coping strategies, new language for your experience, and a clearer understanding of how past and present experiences shape the way you move through life.

What is therapy used for?

People come to therapy when something feels too heavy, confusing, painful, repetitive, or hard to manage alone. Sometimes the concern has a diagnosis. Sometimes the first words are simply, “I am not doing well,” “I feel stuck,” or “I do not know why I keep doing this.”

Stonebridge clinical focus.

Stonebridge is especially oriented toward therapy that looks beneath the symptom list: the pattern, the relationship, the adaptation, the loss, the shame, the protection, and the part of the person that has been trying to keep going.

Trauma and shame

Working with fear, numbness, avoidance, anger, grief, self-blame, and patterns shaped by overwhelming experiences.

Relationships and attachment

Understanding conflict, loneliness, trust, boundaries, closeness, withdrawal, people-pleasing, and the wish to feel known.

Identity and self-worth

Making space for self-criticism, perfectionism, uncertainty, disconnection, identity development, and feeling unseen.

Adolescent therapy

Support for teens navigating anxiety, mood, school pressure, family stress, identity, relationships, and emotional regulation.

Substance use concerns

Outpatient therapy for substance use concerns when telehealth is appropriate and the person does not need a higher level of care.

Stress, burnout, and transition

Therapy for overwhelm, depletion, grief, role strain, caregiving, young adulthood, career change, and periods of personal reorganization.

When Stonebridge may fit.

This is an outpatient psychotherapy practice. The first step is not a commitment to treatment; it is a brief request so the clinical fit, setting, and next step can be considered responsibly.

Good fit

Teens and adults located in Illinois who can attend telehealth from a private, safe setting and want steady outpatient psychotherapy.

May need another setting

Immediate safety concerns, medical instability, severe eating disorder symptoms, active crisis needs, or situations requiring intensive or in-person care may need a different level of support.

Not this pathway

Psychological testing, court/legal evaluations, forensic services, custody-related opinions, disability evaluations, and emergency care are not offered through the psychotherapy waitlist.

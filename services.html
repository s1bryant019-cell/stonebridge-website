Ways to work with Stonebridge

Different pathways, held within one thoughtful clinical frame.

The right format depends on who is seeking care, what is happening, and where change needs to occur. A brief consultation helps determine fit before an intake or group enrollment is confirmed.

01 · Individual care

Adult Therapy

Relational psychotherapy for adults navigating anxiety, depression, trauma responses, grief, identity concerns, burnout, relationship patterns, or substance-use concerns when outpatient care is appropriate.

02 · Developmentally responsive care

Adolescent Therapy

Psychotherapy for teens that considers development, privacy, autonomy, family context, and caregiver involvement according to age, safety, treatment need, and shared goals.

03 · Relationship care

Couples Therapy

Therapy for partners seeking to understand recurring conflict, communication breakdowns, disconnection, trust injuries, major transitions, or patterns that keep the relationship stuck.

04 · Family-system care

Family Therapy

Therapy focused on interaction patterns within the family system, particularly when stress, conflict, transitions, or one person’s symptoms are affecting the family more broadly.

05 · Caregiver support

Parent Support

Individual consultation, parent-focused and family sessions, and educational classes or small groups for parents seeking greater understanding, steadier boundaries, and more workable responses.

06 · Shared therapeutic work

Group Therapy

Clinician-led groups organized around a defined population, concern, or treatment purpose. One recovery-focused group is currently being developed, and prospective participants may express interest.

Fit, format, and coverage

Individual, couples, family, and parent-focused services are structured according to the identified treatment need and the people participating in care. Insurance coverage varies by service and plan; educational classes and some groups may be private-pay. Scope, fees, and coverage are reviewed before services begin.

Concerns often addressed

Problems rarely arrive one at a time.

Stonebridge looks for the pattern beneath the list rather than forcing experience into a single isolated category.

Trauma, stress, and shame

Threat responses, trust, body-based distress, grief, self-worth, and protective patterns shaped by difficult experiences.

Mood, anxiety, and regulation

Depression, panic, irritability, numbness, shutdown, avoidance, emotional intensity, or feeling overwhelmed.

Relationships, identity, and grief

Conflict, disconnection, attachment patterns, people-pleasing, identity questions, loss, and difficulty naming needs.

Coping, substance use, and stuck patterns

Burnout, stress, substance-use concerns when outpatient care fits, and cycles that have not changed through effort alone.

How Stonebridge works

Thoughtful care with enough structure to be useful.

The frame around therapy supports the work without reducing it to a formula.

Relationship

The relationship matters.

Therapy is not reduced to a checklist. The therapeutic relationship is part of the work.

Readiness

Pace and readiness matter.

Safety, consent, timing, and the client’s capacity shape how the work proceeds.

Application

Insight can lead to action.

Skills, structure, and planning may be integrated when they support meaningful change.

Fit and scope

Stonebridge provides outpatient telehealth care. Emergency needs, psychological testing, legal or court-related evaluations, and concerns requiring a higher level of care use different pathways.

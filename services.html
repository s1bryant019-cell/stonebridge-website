Where therapy may help.

When symptoms, relationships, or old protections start narrowing life, therapy can create room to understand what is happening and practice something different.

At Stonebridge, psychotherapy is steady, relational work. Sessions may include reflection, emotional processing, pattern recognition, and practical tools when structure is useful.

The aim is not to force quick answers. It is to make the problem more understandable, less isolating, and more workable.

Care works best when it has enough steadiness to be honest.

Clinical style.

Stonebridge is oriented toward relational, insight-focused therapy that looks beneath the symptom list: patterns, adaptations, losses, shame, fear, and the ways people learned to protect themselves.

When helpful, sessions may also include skills, structure, and planning.

  • Relational The therapy relationship is part of the work.
  • Trauma-informed Pace, safety, and readiness matter.
  • Practical when needed Skills can support insight and change.

Fit and scope.

Stonebridge provides outpatient telehealth psychotherapy. Some needs require a different level or type of care.

May fit

Teens and adults located in Illinois who can meet by telehealth from a private, safe setting and are seeking ongoing outpatient psychotherapy.

Not this pathway

  • Emergency or immediate safety needs.
  • Psychological testing, legal, court-related, custody, or disability evaluations.
  • Needs requiring intensive outpatient, partial hospitalization, residential, inpatient, or in-person care.

When Stonebridge is not the right fit, referral direction may be discussed when possible.

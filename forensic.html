Forensic Services

Forensic services as a future practice area.

Forensic services remain part of the broader Stonebridge practice model, but they are not currently available for new requests. This page describes the intended professional stance for future forensic work.

Current availability

Stonebridge is not currently accepting forensic service requests. Psychotherapy inquiries may still be submitted through the consultation form.

The nature of forensic work

When available, forensic services differ from psychotherapy and general clinical assessment. They require a more defined evaluative frame, greater attention to referral questions, and clarity about the purpose and context of the work being requested.

Structured and professionally defined evaluations
Careful attention to referral questions and scope
Clear boundaries between treatment and evaluation roles

What may be considered

Forensic inquiries may involve requests for assessment, consultation, record review, or more specialized evaluative services depending on the nature of the case and the specific referral question.

Why scope matters

Forensic work depends heavily on context, role, and scope. If this service area becomes available, requests will require careful review before any evaluative work is accepted.

Professional fit and referrals

Not all forensic requests are appropriate for every setting. If a request is outside the intended scope of the practice, thoughtful referral guidance may still be offered when useful.

Current status

Stonebridge is not currently accepting forensic inquiries. The forensic page remains available for orientation, but the consultation form will indicate that this service is unavailable if selected.

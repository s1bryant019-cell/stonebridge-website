Stonebridge is not currently offering forensic services for new requests. The consultation form is configured to prevent forensic submissions so the site does not imply active availability.

The page remains visible because forensic evaluation and consultation are part of the broader professional model and may be developed later with the appropriate referral, documentation, and role-clarity systems in place.

The visual language is intentionally formal: records, structure, assessment material, and architecture rather than courtroom symbolism.
Role Clarity

Evaluation is not advocacy in disguise.

Future forensic work at Stonebridge should be careful about role, referral question, limits of data, and the difference between clinical care and forensic opinion. The evaluator’s task is not to become an advocate, but to answer a defined question with professional integrity.

01

Clear referral question and scope before accepting a matter.

02

Careful attention to limits, data sources, and role boundaries.

03

Reports and opinions grounded in evidence, not persuasion.

Scope

Careful work starts before the evaluation begins.

Forensic services require screening for role conflict, referral fit, records, deadlines, and whether the requested opinion is clinically and ethically appropriate. A forensic question should be defined before any evaluation proceeds.

Role boundaries

Forensic work is distinct from therapy and should not blur treatment with legal opinion.

Defined questions

The referral question should be specific enough to evaluate responsibly.

Limits stated clearly

Good forensic writing makes uncertainty and limits visible, rather than hiding them.

Limits

Not open for scheduling.

This page is informational. It does not indicate that forensic services are available. If you are seeking psychotherapy, use the consultation form. If you are seeking forensic services specifically, Stonebridge is not currently accepting those requests.

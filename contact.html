What to include in an initial inquiry

Over time, this section can become a more concrete inquiry guide rather than a general contact page. The goal is to help prospective clients provide enough information for an informed first response, while still keeping the process simple and approachable.

Basic contact details

  • Name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Preferred way to be contacted

Practical information

  • Current insurance or payment questions
  • Scheduling availability
  • General location if relevant
  • Whether the inquiry is for therapy, assessment, or consultation

Reason for seeking services

  • A brief description of current concerns
  • What the person is hoping help with
  • Any urgency or time-sensitive needs
  • Whether they are looking for short-term or longer-term work

Fit and next steps

  • Whether Stonebridge seems clinically appropriate
  • Whether a consultation should be scheduled
  • Whether referral options may be more useful
  • How to proceed after the first reply
This does not need to become a full intake form right away. For now, it can function as a more thoughtful consultation request page that explains the process clearly and sets expectations for what a prospective client should send in an initial message.

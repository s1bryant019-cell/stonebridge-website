Individual Therapy

Therapy for adults and adolescents.

Stonebridge offers thoughtful individual psychotherapy for Illinois adults and teens who want to understand recurring patterns, respond differently to difficult emotions, and make changes that feel meaningful rather than temporary.

Start Here View Adult & Adolescent Therapy

One-to-one care

The work is organized around the individual client’s history, goals, relationships, and current needs.

Relational and practical

Insight, emotional understanding, and usable strategies are integrated rather than treated as competing approaches.

Illinois telehealth

Secure online sessions for adults and adolescents who are located in Illinois.

A treatment frame shaped around the person

Different stages of life require different kinds of support.

Individual therapy provides a consistent setting to make sense of what is happening internally and relationally. The work may focus on symptoms, difficult experiences, repeated patterns, important relationships, identity, decision-making, or the ways a person has learned to protect themselves.

Treatment is not delivered as a fixed formula. Relational and psychodynamic understanding may be combined with cognitive, behavioral, acceptance-based, emotion-regulation, motivational, trauma-informed, attachment-focused, or addiction-focused methods according to the client’s formulation and readiness.

Adult Therapy

A place to understand what keeps repeating—and what could change.

Adult therapy can hold both immediate concerns and the longer patterns beneath them. The goal is not only to reduce distress, but to develop a clearer understanding of experience and more flexible ways of responding.

Mood and anxiety

Depression, worry, panic, irritability, or emotional shutdown

Therapy can help clarify what intensifies distress, how it is managed, and what supports greater regulation and choice.

Trauma and stress

Protective responses that remain active after difficult experiences

The work proceeds with attention to safety, pacing, consent, body-based responses, trust, and the meaning attached to what happened.

Relationships

Conflict, disconnection, attachment patterns, or difficulty naming needs

Individual therapy can examine how earlier and current relationships shape expectations, boundaries, closeness, and self-protection.

Identity and transition

Grief, burnout, role changes, identity questions, or feeling directionless

Therapy offers space to consider what has changed, what has been lost, and what kind of life feels more consistent with the person’s values.

Coping patterns

Avoidance, overwork, people-pleasing, compulsive behavior, or self-criticism

Patterns are understood as adaptations before they are challenged, making room for alternatives that are more sustainable and less costly.

Substance use

Substance-use concerns when outpatient psychotherapy is appropriate

Care may address motivation, triggers, emotional and relational patterns, relapse prevention, recovery support, and coordination when additional services are needed.

Adolescent Therapy

Care for the teen as a person—not simply as a problem to solve.

01 · Development

Understand the concern within the adolescent’s stage of development.

Identity, independence, belonging, school, family expectations, peer relationships, and rapidly changing emotions are considered together rather than in isolation.

02 · Relationship

Create enough trust for honest work.

Teens need a therapeutic relationship where they can speak directly, feel taken seriously, and explore difficult experiences without every session becoming another performance for adults.

03 · Privacy

Protect appropriate privacy while keeping caregivers meaningfully involved.

Expectations for confidentiality, safety exceptions, caregiver updates, and parent participation are discussed clearly at the beginning and revisited as treatment develops.

04 · Application

Connect insight to everyday life.

Sessions may include emotional regulation, communication, problem-solving, values, coping strategies, and practical planning when those tools support the larger therapeutic work.

Adolescent therapy has a clear but flexible frame

The adolescent is the primary individual client. Caregivers remain important partners, but parent involvement is structured according to the teen’s age, safety, clinical needs, treatment goals, and applicable privacy requirements. This differs from family therapy, where the relationship system is the primary focus, and from parent support, where the parent is receiving consultation or education.

Consultation before commitment

Fit is considered before an intake is confirmed.

Step 01

Send a brief inquiry

Share a brief description of the concern, whether services are for an adult or adolescent, availability, insurance information when relevant, and what you hope therapy will address.

Step 02

Brief consultation

A free 10–15 minute consultation helps clarify clinical scope, telehealth fit, availability, and whether individual therapy appears to be the appropriate next step.

Step 03

Initial assessment

Early sessions develop a fuller understanding of symptoms, history, relationships, strengths, risks, goals, and the treatment structure. For adolescents, caregiver participation and privacy expectations are also clarified.

Step 04

Ongoing individualized work

Treatment develops through a steady therapeutic relationship, with approaches and pacing adjusted according to formulation, progress, readiness, and changing needs.

Fees and coverage

Insurance coverage depends on the client’s plan, eligibility, clinical circumstances, and the service provided. Benefits, fees, and any private-pay responsibility are reviewed before ongoing treatment begins.

Outpatient scope and safety

Individual therapy is not emergency response, crisis stabilization, psychological testing, legal evaluation, or a substitute for a higher level of care. Safety, substance use, medical needs, current supports, and telehealth appropriateness are considered during the fit review and throughout treatment.

Begin with a brief fit review.

Share whether you are seeking adult or adolescent therapy so scope, availability, telehealth appropriateness, and next steps can be considered carefully.

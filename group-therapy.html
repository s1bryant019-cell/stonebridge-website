Group Therapy

A steady group can make difficult change less isolating.

Stonebridge is developing structured, clinician-led therapy groups for people who may benefit from shared work within a thoughtful, clearly held frame. Groups are not currently enrolling, but prospective participants may express interest while offerings are finalized.

Why group therapy

Insight develops differently when it is shared.

Group therapy offers something individual work cannot fully reproduce: the opportunity to notice patterns, practice new ways of relating, and receive thoughtful feedback within a consistent community. Members bring different histories, but the group is organized around a shared clinical purpose.

The frame matters. Stonebridge groups will be intentionally composed, clearly structured, and facilitated with attention to safety, participation, boundaries, and the needs of the group as a whole. Group therapy is not simply individual therapy conducted in front of other people.

One group in development

Recovery, relapse prevention, and rebuilding daily life.

Structured recovery-focused group

For adults seeking a steadier approach to recovery.

One potential offering is a clinician-led psychotherapy group for adults working to understand patterns related to substance use, strengthen practical recovery skills, and build a life that supports continued change. The group would combine focused discussion, reflection, skills practice, and attention to relationships rather than relying on a single fixed formula.

  • Triggers, cravings, and high-risk situations
  • Emotional regulation and coping under stress
  • Daily structure, routines, and meaningful activity
  • Relapse warning signs and prevention planning
  • Trust, communication, and relationship repair
  • Support, accountability, and continued care

The final title, schedule, duration, fee, eligibility criteria, and opening date have not yet been set. Expressing interest does not guarantee enrollment or establish a treatment relationship.

What the group frame offers

Shared work with individual relevance.

A well-designed group creates enough structure for members to work honestly without losing sight of their own goals, circumstances, and responsibilities.

Recognition

Less isolation

Hearing how others understand and navigate similar struggles can reduce shame without minimizing individual differences.

Perspective

More than one viewpoint

Members can consider patterns and choices through perspectives that may not emerge in individual reflection alone.

Practice

Relationships in real time

The group provides a place to notice communication, avoidance, trust, boundaries, and repair as they occur between people.

Continuity

A steady therapeutic frame

Regular attendance, clear expectations, and a consistent purpose help members return to the work over time.

How participation will work

Interest comes before enrollment.

Step 01

Share your interest.

Prospective participants may send a brief inquiry describing the kind of group support they are seeking and any scheduling considerations.

Step 02

Complete a fit conversation.

Before a group opens, a brief consultation will clarify the group’s purpose, current needs, telehealth suitability, and whether another level or type of care may be more appropriate.

Step 03

Review readiness and composition.

Group placement depends on clinical fit, readiness to participate, scheduling, and a composition that can support useful work for all members.

Step 04

Confirm the frame before commitment.

Schedule, attendance expectations, confidentiality, fee, group length, and participation agreements are reviewed before enrollment is confirmed.

Scope and boundaries

Group therapy is not crisis care, detoxification, withdrawal management, an intensive outpatient program, medical addiction treatment, or a substitute for a higher level of care when one is needed. Privacy and respectful participation are required, but confidentiality in a group also depends on each member honoring the agreements reviewed before enrollment.

Availability and fees

No Stonebridge therapy group is currently enrolling. Schedule, format, group size, fee, and possible insurance coverage will be stated clearly before a group opens so prospective members can make an informed decision before committing.

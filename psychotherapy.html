Psychotherapy

Therapy with depth, steadiness, and fit.

Psychotherapy is the current active service at Stonebridge. The work is intended for people seeking a thoughtful clinical relationship, careful attention to patterns, and a setting that values fit rather than volume.

Currently available

Current psychotherapy services.

Stonebridge is currently accepting psychotherapy inquiries. Therapy is offered through a careful initial review process rather than immediate open scheduling. The goal is to determine whether the clinical question, timing, and format appear appropriate for the practice.

This protects the client’s time and vulnerability while also preserving the integrity of the work. Not every inquiry will be the right fit, and that clarity is part of the clinical frame.

The visual frame stays quiet on purpose: enough warmth to feel human, enough structure to feel contained.
Clinical Stance

Not just symptom management.

Therapy at Stonebridge is informed by depth-oriented clinical thinking. Symptoms matter, but they are understood in context: patterns of feeling, relationships, meaning, history, defenses, identity, and the ways people organize themselves around distress.

Format

Telehealth psychotherapy.

Psychotherapy is currently offered via telehealth for clients located in Illinois. Telehealth work still requires a serious frame: privacy, consistency, attention, and a setting where the client can speak freely.

01. Initial inquiry

Submit the consultation form with a brief, non-emergency description of what brings you to seek therapy.

02. Review for fit

If the request appears appropriate for Stonebridge, follow-up will occur by email.

03. Consultation

A brief consultation may help clarify needs, expectations, and whether ongoing therapy is the right next step.

Fit

Good care requires the right setting.

Stonebridge is not meant to be a high-volume clinic. Fit matters clinically, ethically, and practically. When the practice is not the right setting, the response should be direct, respectful, and useful rather than vague.

Starting Therapy

Begin with an initial inquiry.

Use the consultation form for non-emergency psychotherapy inquiries. Please keep the initial description brief and avoid detailed clinical, legal, or highly sensitive information.

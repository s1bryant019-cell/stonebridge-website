Psychotherapy

Telehealth psychotherapy with room to think.

Stonebridge currently offers outpatient telehealth psychotherapy for adults located in Illinois. The work is consultation-first, fit-conscious, and structured around sustainable, attentive care.

Outpatient care

Not a crisis, emergency, or higher-level-care service.

What therapy may focus on

  • Anxiety, stress, and emotional overwhelm
  • Depression, grief, and adjustment concerns
  • Relationship patterns and identity questions
  • Trauma-informed work when outpatient telehealth is appropriate
  • Burnout, professional strain, and life transitions

How the process begins

The first step is not open booking. Stonebridge uses a brief consultation to understand what you are looking for, whether telehealth is clinically appropriate, and whether the practice is likely to be a good fit.

If Stonebridge is not the right setting, referral guidance may be offered when possible.

Session structure

Therapy appointments are scheduled in 60-minute blocks, with the clinical portion generally lasting 45–50 minutes.

Communication

Routine communication is not intended for emergencies and should remain brief, appropriate, and clinically relevant.

Emergency boundary

Stonebridge is not an emergency service. Immediate danger or medical emergencies require 911 or the nearest emergency room; crisis support is available by calling or texting 988.

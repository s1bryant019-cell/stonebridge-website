What therapy is like at Stonebridge

Therapy is not organized around performance, quick labels, or one-size-fits-all scripts. The work may explore how a person learned to survive, what now feels limiting, and how old protections show up in current relationships, identity, self-worth, grief, anxiety, trauma responses, or substance use concerns.

Practical tools may be used when they support the work. The broader aim is to create enough steadiness that complexity can be understood without reducing the client to symptoms or diagnosis alone.

Who this may fit

  • Teens and adults located in Illinois
  • Clients seeking outpatient telehealth psychotherapy
  • Concerns involving anxiety, trauma responses, grief, identity, relationships, burnout, substance use, or life transitions
  • Clients who want therapy to feel thoughtful, steady, and clinically grounded

How the process begins

The first step is consultation, not open booking. The consultation helps clarify fit, scope, risk level, and whether care can begin responsibly in this setting.

Session structure

Therapy appointments are scheduled in 60-minute blocks, with the direct clinical portion generally lasting 45–50 minutes.

Care limits

Stonebridge is not an emergency, crisis, or higher-level-care service. If another level of care is more appropriate, referral guidance may be offered when possible.
